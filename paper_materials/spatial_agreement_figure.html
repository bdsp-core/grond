Figure A. Pairwise Jaccard agreement matrices at three binarization thresholds. Rater-rater entries (LB-PH, LB-SZ, PH-SZ) are threshold-invariant since rater labels are binary. At threshold 0.38 (left, green border), the model's average agreement with LB and PH (0.767) exceeds the LB-PH inter-rater agreement (0.762). N = 220 segments with 3-rater ground truth.

B. Agreement Comparison

Figure B. Left: Mean model-rater Jaccard across all 3 raters at each threshold, compared to the mean rater-rater baseline (0.751). Right: All six pairwise agreements (3 rater-rater + 3 model-rater), grouped by threshold. The model achieves its highest agreement with PH (0.842 at threshold 0.38), exceeding all rater-rater pairs.

C. Threshold Optimization Sweep

Figure C. avg(Model-LB, Model-PH) Jaccard as a function of binarization threshold (green solid line), with mean model-all-raters shown in yellow. Horizontal dashed lines indicate LB-PH inter-rater agreement (0.762, green) and mean rater-rater agreement (0.751, gray). A broad plateau exists from 0.25 to 0.40, with the optimal threshold at 0.38. Agreement drops sharply above 0.45, indicating the model's continuous probability outputs are well-calibrated in the 0.25–0.40 range.
