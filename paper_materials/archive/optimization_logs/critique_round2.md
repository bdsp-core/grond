These EEG characterization figures have a strong foundation, with clear data presentation and a logical layout. However, several critical improvements are needed to meet the high standards of a top neurology journal, primarily concerning readability and optimal use of space.

Here are the top 7 most impactful improvements, focusing on visual presentation and styling:

1.  **Significantly Increase Font Sizes for All Labels and Descriptions:** This is the most critical and pervasive issue. Almost all text elements are too small for print readability.
    *   **Channel Labels (EEG, e.g., Fp1-F7):** Increase to 8-9pt.
    *   **Time Axis Labels (0, 2, 4... 10 s) and "Time (s)" label:** Increase to 8-9pt.
    *   **Verbal Descriptions (e.g., "LPD at 2.5 Hz, bilateral..."):** Increase to 8-9pt. These are currently very difficult to read.
    *   **Topoplot Electrode Labels (e.g., Fp1, Fp2):** Increase significantly to 7-8pt. They are currently almost invisible.
    *   **Colorbar Labels (0.45, 0.50...) and "Score" label:** Increase to 7-8pt.
    *   **Difficulty Badges (EASY, MEDIUM, HARD):** Increase font size and potentially make them bold for better visibility, ensuring they remain clear but not dominant.

2.  **Adjust Vertical Spacing Between Rows:** Increase the vertical margin/padding between the bottom of one example's verbal description and the top of the next example's EEG plot. This will improve visual separation between the three examples within each figure, making them less crowded.

3.  **Optimize Topoplot Size and Position:**
    *   Slightly increase the overall size of the topoplots to make better use of the allocated horizontal space. This will also naturally improve the readability of the electrode labels (once their font size is increased).
    *   Ensure the head outline is always fully visible and not cut off by the plot boundaries, especially at the top.

4.  **Refine Difficulty Badges:** While the text needs to be larger (as per point 1), consider making the background of the badges slightly more distinct (e.g., a slightly darker shade or a subtle border) to make them pop a little more without being dominant. Ensure the text color contrasts well with the badge background.

5.  **Increase EEG Plot Right Margin:** Slightly increase the right margin of the EEG plots to provide a bit more breathing room between the EEG traces and the topoplots, preventing them from appearing too close.

6.  **Ensure Absolute Cross-Figure Consistency:** While the current figures are largely consistent in their *problematic* styling, it is paramount that *all* the proposed improvements (font sizes, spacing, badge appearance, etc.) are applied *identically* across all four figures (LPD, GPD, LRDA, GRDA). This is a non-negotiable requirement for a professional publication.

7.  **Review Main Title Placement:** The main title ("LPD Characterization Examples") is currently quite high, leaving a large gap above the first EEG plot. Consider lowering it slightly to reduce this empty space, making the overall figure more compact and efficient for column width publication.

By addressing these points, the figures will become significantly more readable, professional, and suitable for publication in a high-impact neurology journal. The core data presentation is strong; it's the surrounding elements that need refinement.
